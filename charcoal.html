Source & Origin

Sourced from the
Heart of Namibia

Our charcoal is harvested from the Oshikoto Region of Namibia — one of Southern Africa's most resource-rich hardwood areas. The lump charcoal is produced from Black Thorn and Mushara Tree, two of the densest hardwoods in the region, resulting in an exceptionally high fixed carbon content and long, clean burn.

Our briquettes are made from Dichrostachys cinerea — known locally as Sickle Bush — an invasive species whose controlled removal actively benefits the surrounding ecosystem. Every bag of briquettes is part of an ongoing land restoration effort.

All products are sourced through our exclusive Southern Africa partner, Gen Y Solutions PTY Ltd, with full chain-of-custody documentation available on request.

Sustainability Story

A Product with a Purpose

Most charcoal brands can't tell you what wood they use. We can — and the story behind our briquettes is a genuine differentiator in today's eco-conscious retail market.

01
Invasive Species Removal
Our briquettes are made from Sickle Bush (Dichrostachys cinerea) — an invasive species that degrades Namibian grazing land. Every purchase directly supports its removal and the restoration of native ecosystems.
02
Full Traceability
Unlike most imported charcoal, we can tell you exactly what tree species was used, what region it came from, and who processed it. That traceability is increasingly valuable to retailers, Amazon sellers, and eco-conscious buyers.
03
Zero Chemical Additives
No petroleum binders, no chemical accelerants, no fillers. The briquettes are held together with 8% natural maize starch only — fully carbonized, bark free, and clean burning from first light to last ember.
